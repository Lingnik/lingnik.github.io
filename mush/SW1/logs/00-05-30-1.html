Log Date: May 30, 2000

People Involved: Vauki, Zeak

Synopsis: With a bit of frustration, the team is kicked off Dreven, with only a little bit more information than when they left... particularly knowledge of a huge fleet orbiting the planet.

Background: After only being on Dreven for a few hours, the team is rounded up to the starport, only to meet Zeak...

Zeak's Description: He is wearing a thick armored flight suit. It covers his whole body, except his his head, under a set of black metalloid pieces which sit on top of a black flight suit. Tiny servo motors have been placed in it, to help it's wearer move about unencumbered. An thin armored box on the front holds life support, as well as radio equipment. On his right breast, a dark insignia, almost untoticable indicates this individual to be an officer in the Imperial Navy holding the rank of: Commander. A blaster sits holstered flush against his left abdomen, and a short range weapon, but more powerful than the blaster sits holstered against his right thigh. Anything heavier would sit in or on the light backpack slung on his back. A flight helmet, again coated with armor, sits on top of the outfit. It resembles the normal TIE flight helmet, except that it has a single eye piece that extends around his entire frontal viewing arc, and is a bit taller than normal. Vauki's Description: A mat of brown frazzled hair lies atop this man's head. His face, worn softly, is still young, the lines still soft, but some amount of scarring showing over a small amount of it. A black vest rests around his upper body, enwrapping a plain white shirt; each being a little worn, threads coming loose from the edges, and some small burns at other locations. Sleeves, also black, lie along his arms from the vest, very loosely hung... perfect for hiding something. A simple silver chain rests loosely around his neck, which leads down into the shirt, a small bump created a few inches down from some unseen item. His pants consist of a pair of loose black slacks, void of anything interesting, but upon which rests a thick belt of some unknown material. At the base of his legs, his feet lie in black leather, almost militaristic boots. The flat black material gives off no shine, and reach nearly a foot up his leg. The crew of the Triton had been assembled over the last hour, the last parts of her put back together. Her 'captain' is being held as a 'guest' of the Empire outside by a pair of troopers. While they are not being forceful, they make it clear that if he moves, there will be consequences. Down the ramp of a recently landed lambda strolls Zeak . . . covered in his heavy field armor. He looks over the troops then begins approaching the Triton.

Standing there, in the midst of the pair of guards, Mege glowers around slowly, making quite sure his disapproval of the current situation is seen by all. He almost eminates a blood-red anger. His eyes flick onto Zeak suddenly, and follow him. He makes not a single sound, content in his own ways to simply glower.

When he gets within a polite distance, Zeak finally speaks, his voice distorted slightly by the helmet com, but not enough to remove the extreme agitation he is feeling at something or some one, "You and your crew are here by ordered to leave the system. I don't know what you intended to smuggle off this rock . . . but I don't have time to deal with it or you." As he speaks two troopers begin loading some familiar looking containers into the ship. "You will find you ability to navigate unimpaired again, and your cargo intact . . . I don't care where you go but if you come back here you will be afforded neither the chance to land or jump out. Do you understand me Captain?"

"I understand completely, /Commander/." Vauki responds quite diplomatically, save for the final word, which is emphasized quite harshly. "I don't think I'd want to come back." Finally, Mege seems to relent his bad mood, and takes on a more nicer tone of voice, "I apologize that I and my companions were such an inconvenience to you... I thank you for the repairs you offered, though." The change in mood might actually be seen as sickening... for one so blind with rage to suddenly become so friendly and warm.

"Your ship has been cleared through traffic, an escort will meet you in space," Zeak continues, his tone slightly less harsh. "You are to jump as soon as you clear the well." With that he turns and signals to the storm troopers who with him begin to withdraw.

Mege nods softly, his stance taking a more relaxed position as the troopers move away from his vehicle, "Thank you again, so much, for your assistance... I'm very glad we managed to fall into your hands. Better than having to be stranded for eternity." A smile plays forth on his face, and he bobs his head out of respect, "Farewell, Commander."

Being in much too foul of a mood to be polite, not so much at the Captain but at his superiors for the repremand he recieved for not following orders they never gave, Zeak ignores the man's civility and continues to walk away. He stops and looks back only when he and his troops have cleared the saftey marker for the ships ion engines.

Vauki almost shrugs at the lack of response, and he himself turns towards his ship. He trods forth, the rest of his team already aboard, and makes his way up the loading ramp. Slapping at the control panel as he reaches the top, the ramp slowly withdraws into the ship, melding into the mess of components. After a few minutes, her engines begin to burn fully, dust floating up around behind her.
